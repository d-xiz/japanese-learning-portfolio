Introduction

Guided Learning Overview

Guided Learning is a self-directed learning that allows students to explore a topic of personal interest while following a structured learning framework making the outcome more fruitfull. Instead of traditional classroom-led instruction, students take ownership of their learning by planning objectives, doing activities which enhance learning, progress tracking, reflecting on outcomesand modification learning procedure with respect to facilitators advice.

This project follows the Plan-Perform-Monitor-Reflect (PPMR) framework, which ensures that learning is intentional, trackable, and reflective. The framework encourages continuous improvement through regular self-evaluation and adjustment throughout the learning process.

Learning Project Background

This Guided Learning project focuses on learning basic Japanese language words and phrase with an exploration of Japanese anime culture and its global influence. The project was designed to combine language learning with cultural research, allowing both areas to combine togather and reinforce each other.

Anime was chosen as the cultural focus because of its widespread international reach and its role in shaping perceptions of Japanese language, lifestyle and tourism.

üìã PLAN - My Goals and Planning Decisions

Initial Goals: I planned to learn basic Japanese communication skills while researching anime's global cultural impact. My decision to combine language learning with cultural research was strategic - I believed understanding the language would enhance my appreciation of anime's cultural nuances.

Planning Decisions: I chose Duolingo for structured language learning because of its gamified approach and progress tracking. For research, I planned to use academic sources, industry reports, and case studies. My 7-week timeline was designed to allow for gradual skill building while maintaining momentum.

Resource Planning: I allocated 30 minutes daily for Duolingo practice, 2 hours weekly for research, and 1 hour weekly for reflection and documentation. This balanced approach ensured steady progress in both areas.

‚ö° PERFORM - Actions I Took

Language Learning Actions: I consistently practiced Japanese daily using Duolingo, achieving a 49-day streak. I created flashcards for vocabulary retention, recorded myself speaking weekly to track pronunciation improvement, and practiced conversations with Japanese exchange students online.

Research Actions: I systematically researched anime's cultural impact by analyzing academic papers, industry reports, and case studies. I documented findings in organized notes, created mind maps to connect different cultural influences, and interviewed peers about their anime-influenced behaviors.

Documentation Actions: I maintained weekly reflection journals, took screenshots of progress, saved drafts of all work, and organized evidence systematically for portfolio compilation.

üìä MONITOR - Challenges, Feedback, and Adjustments

Challenges Encountered: Japanese pronunciation proved more difficult than expected, particularly the subtle differences between similar sounds. Research initially felt overwhelming due to the vast amount of available information about anime's global impact.

Feedback Received: My language exchange partner noted improvement in my pronunciation after week 4. Peer feedback on my research highlighted the need for more quantitative data to support cultural impact claims.

Adjustments Made: I increased pronunciation practice by listening to native speakers on YouTube and repeating phrases. For research, I focused on specific, measurable impacts rather than general observations. I also adjusted my study schedule to allocate more time to challenging areas.

üí≠ REFLECT - What Worked, What Didn't, and Improvements

What Worked Well: The combination of language learning and cultural research created synergistic learning - understanding Japanese helped me appreciate anime dialogue nuances, while anime research provided cultural context for language learning. Daily Duolingo practice built strong habits, and weekly recordings clearly showed progress.

What Didn't Work: Initially, I underestimated the time needed for quality research. My first attempts at pronunciation were frustrating, and I nearly gave up on speaking practice. Some research sources were too academic and difficult to synthesize.

Key Learning Outcomes: Beyond language skills and cultural knowledge, I developed stronger self-directed learning abilities, improved research skills, and better time management. I learned the importance of consistent practice and the value of iterative improvement.

Future Improvements: For future projects, I would allocate more time for skill development phases and seek feedback earlier in the process. I would also incorporate more diverse learning methods from the beginning rather than adding them as adjustments.

Personal Growth: This project significantly boosted my confidence in self-directed learning and cross-cultural understanding. I'm now more motivated to continue Japanese language study and pursue deeper cultural research projects.
